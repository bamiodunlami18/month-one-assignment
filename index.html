Even in retail, I find ways to bring innovation into my work, whether it’s through problem-solving,
process optimization, or understanding how technology can enhance efficiency. My passion lies in building

As I continue my journey, I’m focused on growing my skills in backend development, learning new technologies,
and one day making a meaningful impact in the tech industry. For me, innovation isn’t just a buzzword, it’s a mindset.


WHY ALTSCHOOL AFRICA

I first came across Altschool Africa, a diploma-awarding tech learning center, through a tweet by Hacksultan on X.
He shared inspiring stories of people who had enrolled, learned valuable skills, and landed jobs at top organizations.
Intrigued, I decided to check out the platform Altschool Africa.

At first, I wasn’t sure which tech path to take, I just knew that I loved coding. To find clarity,
I conducted research and took personal assessments to determine whether frontend or backend engineering suited me better.

One thing about me, I’m an introvert. I prefer working behind the scenes, focusing on structure and functionality rather
than what’s loud or flashy. That realization became the foundation for my decision to pursue backend engineering,
where I can build the core systems that power applications while staying in my element.

BACKEND ENGINEERING

I chose backend engineering because it aligns with my strengths and interests. As someone who prefers working behind the scenes,
I love the idea of building the core functionality of applications, the part that makes everything work. Backend developers are
the backbone of any software system, handling databases, servers, and APIs, ensuring everything runs smoothly.

Another key factor in my decision was the depth of knowledge backend engineers have. They work with multiple programming languages,
databases, and system architectures, making them highly skilled problem-solvers. I wanted to challenge myself and gain a solid
foundation in complex technical concepts.

Of course, I can’t ignore the salary aspect—backend engineers generally earn more than frontend developers, which was an added
motivation for me. The combination of technical depth, problem-solving, and financial reward made backend engineering the perfect
choice for me.
